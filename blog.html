Tacos Perrones

Callejon Quintana Roo 1511, Zona Urbana Rio Tijuana, 22010 Tijuana, B.C., Mexico. Open 24/7.

Grilled meat or go home....

After spending the better part of a year making sushi 6-7 days a week, 12-16 hours a day, I've been on a sabbatical in Mexico as of late. I've been ruminating over the writings of Jim Harrison, my purpose in life, boxing at the many gyms on offer here, and in between moments of substantial happiness and sad mopery, I find the time to indulge in the exploration of the great cuisine this country has to offer.

This is one of my all time favorite taco spots in Tijuana.

Open 24/7, probably the only place to be open in this manner. You can find the hedonistic pursuits of alcohol or strippers in Tijuana at any hour of the day and night, but tacos have a closing time.

Not here. You won't have to settle for McDonalds or 7/11 taquitos to hold you over til everything opens back up at 8AM.

Having driven by this place multiple times, I was initally iffy about going to a spot located in a strip mall, as the best tacos I've found are usually hole in the walls or stand alone carts. However hunger hit at 5AM, and as Las Ahumaderas closes at 4AM on weekdays, I decided to give it a shot.

Upon parking, I was pleasantly suprised to find that they were actually grilling meat over firewood outside which was eerie given the hour. Usually BBQs are winding down at ths time, but here this guy was single handedly grilling 10s of lbs of meat in batches. He then transferred the grilled goods into pans and chopped it up inside. Great sign, as most places use charcoal around here (still a great medium).

Firewood gives meat that extra OOOOMPH. It is the way meat is meant to be eaten; cooked until charred in all the right places with crispy bits. Gas grills are a cop out, and meat is not meant to be heated over thin sheet metal either. I presume cavemen would have gone vegan if this were the case. If you show up to a taqueria and they are cooking meat over a flap top, quickly walk away and spend your money elsewhere, unless if they are making cheesesteaks. In TJ there is no place for anything besides grilled meats. If you are in California, well, that is what you have to live with. So just come down here and eat like a king.

I was sure I was in the right place at this point. After chatting with the server for a bit, I found out they are most famous for their namesake "perrones" taco, which loosely translates to "amazing taco", or "kickass taco"; something along the lines of denoting awesomeness in an exclaimitory fashion. Apparently a long time customer at the original location (this is an outpost) asked for a taco with EVERYTHING in it, and started raining down expletives while chomping down in drunken ecstasy. We've all been there. They chose to go with one of his less offensive, yet colorful descriptory words, "perrones", which I believe was a wise choice.

This is not the first time alcohol has lead to culinary innovation and many a line cook can attest to this. I assume a majority of breakthroughs in food were made when the mind is made pliable enough to try something new. Ask me to make you my Tex-Mex hollandaise burgerdog if we are drunk enough, and prepare for the pinnacle of inebriated dining. Tecate poundings and second helpings to follow.

Rather than a simple taco, the perrones really is a large flour quesadilla, giving it that soft and supple bite that only flour tortillas have, oozing with cheese, stuffed with arrachera (grilled skirt steak), cilantro, onions, salsa, guacamole, pinto beans, and interestingly, tiny bits of crispy bacon. The beans are a great addition and really flesh out the perrones taco with a bit of extra "meatiness" of the vegetable front, which add a creamy contrast to the charred meat.

There is a time and a place to contemplate the intricacies of tripas tacos delicately tempered with salsa, varying from spot to spot depending on their particular taqueria's preperation process. This is not one of those. This is the kind of taco (quesadilla?) you get when you just want pure, unadulterated excess. Total kick in the ass with a one way express ticket to meatgasm. The official outpost is in Rosarito, where apparently an even better version is served. I'll report back once I try.

Regular corn tortilla tacos are also on offer here, and they usually have a deal for 3 corn tortillas with a drink. However the perrones reigns king here and should be the only reason you came.


I recommend coming around noon, as that is when they are grilling a fresh batch of meat to meet the lunch crowd demand. One thing I've noticed is that chicken is not a taco meat offering here in Mexico. I wholeheartedly agree with this sentiment and hope the milquetoasts may find their chicken tacos at their local Baja Fresh and leave the real taco eating to us.

The habanero salsa here is to die for, if you can handle the heat. I personally like to unfold the perrones, add a ton of salsa roja and verde on opposite sides, fold it back up, and and pour on a little habanero salsa as needed in between bites. To douse it completely in their habanero salsa is a bit much and you will overwhelm the perrones with painful heat. However as it is eat it as you like.

Ask for more salsa if you are ordering togo as they can be skimpy with the goods. If you are extra hungry, go for the super perrones, which is basically the perrones but doubled in size and enough to feed you and you again later that night. Wash it all down with a cup of their caldo de res (basically a beef consomme) and that is a meal worth having with no compromise at any hour of the day.
